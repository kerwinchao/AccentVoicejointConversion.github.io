Accent and Speaker Disentanglement in Many-to-many Voice Conversion

Authors: Zhichao Wang, Wenshuo Ge, Xiong Wang,Shan Yang,Wendong Gan,Haitao Chen,Hai Li, Lei Xie
Abstract: Many-to-many accented voice conversion aims to converts the source speech to target speech with different accents from the target speaker. Conventional methods are limited by the strong correlation between accent and speaker, leading to the interaction of timbre and accent. In this paper, we present a many-tomany accented voice conversion method based on the encoder-decoder framework with limited accent data. We achieve this by reducing accent leakage on the ASR model and disentangling accent and speaker identity. The experiments on the data of Mandarin and the Tianjin accent show that the proposed system can achieve the goal of many-to-many accented voice conversion. Our objective evaluations and subjective evaluations show that the conversion model we proposed achieved the best results without hurting audio quality and speaker similarity.
